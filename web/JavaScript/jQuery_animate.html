I SAW THEE’WEEP ——George Gordon Byron I saw thee weep the big bright tear 　　Came out that eye of blue; 　　And then me thought it did appear 　　A violet dropping dew: I see thee smile 　　the sapphire’s blaze 　　Beside thee ceased to shine; 　　It could not match the living rays That filled that glance of thine. 　　As clouds from yonder sun receive 　　A deep and mellow dye 　　Which scarce the shade of coming eve Can banish from the sky, 　　Those smiles unto the moodiest mind 　　Their own pure joy impart; 　　Their sunshine leaves a glow behind 　　That lightens on the heart.
